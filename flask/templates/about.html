How it works?

The model is developed based on Deep Learning algorithm to interpret the chest X-ray images. The model is trained on one of the largest publicly available chest x-ray datasets to scientific community by NIH Clinical Center.

The model uses transfer learning by taking the first layers of a VGG16 model trained on ImageNet data (classifying color images of dogs, airplanes, cats, ...) and retrains it on grayscale images of chests. K-Means clustering has been chosen due to its ability to define the number of clusters and it was shown to be relatively good when examining clustering error metrics.
